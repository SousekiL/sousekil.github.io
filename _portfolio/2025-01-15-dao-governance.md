## Problem

DAOs often rely on both on-chain and off-chain governance, but the two systems do not always produce the same outcomes. This project asks how governance design shapes divergence in participation, voting behavior, and final decisions.

## Data

The analysis combines governance records, proposal-level voting outcomes, and institutional design features from DAO decision processes. The focus is on settings where the same governance question can be compared across different voting mechanisms.

## Method

I use causal and quasi-experimental reasoning to study how rule design and implementation details affect divergence between on-chain and off-chain outcomes. The goal is not only to describe disagreement, but to explain which governance features make divergence more likely.

## Findings

The project shows that governance architecture can materially influence how people participate and how decisions are aggregated. Differences between on-chain and off-chain systems are not only procedural; they can affect perceived legitimacy and the strategic behavior of voters.

## Why It Matters

DAO governance is often treated as transparent by design, but the institutional layer still matters. Understanding vote divergence helps evaluate whether a governance system is robust, representative, and incentive-compatible.
